Resource Guide

History/Archives:

National Museum of the American Indian

NMAI houses one of the largest collections of indigenous artifacts. It is a network of three facilities: the museum on the National Mall in Washington DC, the Cultural Resources Center in Suitland, Maryland, and the George Gustav Heye Center in New York City. The George Gustav Heye Center is also called the National Museum of the American Indian.

New York Public Library resources

Provides access to archives and databases as well as digitized maps and images, but some require a library card.

The New Yorker's piece on Mannahatta

This article serves as a great starting point for examining what New York City was before colonization. It provides a brief history of Mannahatta and focuses on the ecological effects of humans on the land.

A non-academic history book that profiles Native leaders from across the US, with a focus on policy and law and the relationship of indigenous sovereignty to the US Constitution and government.

American Indian Community House

The AICH is a community center serving the Native American Community within New York City. They provide health and youth services, as well as facilitate community events and support local artists.
